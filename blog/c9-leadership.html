What is your experience of compassionate (or not) leadership?

In the past, I worked under someone having the quality of compassionate leadership. He had all the three components of cognitive ("I understand you"), affective ("I feel for you"), and motivational ("I want to help you"). When he was just a Senior Engineer, I didn't see the components from him. As an Intermediate Engineer, I just thought that he was a weak and soft spoken person. And I doubt that he had the capability to manage a team under his soft shell when he was promoted to manage a team. (To put you into perspective, the other managers were like fierce tigers and dragons that won't let go the preys in their mouth. They are the perfect example of not compassionate leadership)

There was a time my team was lacking a manager. The person was assigned to be the manager in my team. When the news broke up that the person will be our manager, one of my colleagues rose his thumb and said that: "Good news!". Well, I doubted his excitement. Anyway, the colleague was someone I respected. So, I took his words and felt hopeful that we will be getting a good manager. The immediate effect I noticed when working for the new manager can be described in one word: "Comfortable". The next thing, I felt that I could trust him and ask for his help when I was in trouble with my work. As we continue working together, I finally can see the secret power behind his soft shell. In one of the projects, the new software tool that I worked on was under the risk of not meeting the schedule, putting the whole project at risk. The first thing he did, was to understand about my situation and the reason of cannot deliver on time. He felt my frustration on the situation and he knew that I was taking blame for the delay in the project. Then, he used his negotiation skills to help me to get agreement from my stakeholders to prioritize the tasks so that some tasks could be delivered after the critical milestone. He helped me went through my career crisis.

My colleague once told me a story regarding what he observed. There was a time when a senior manager meeting with a group of managers to discuss some issues. Whenever the other managers expressing their opinions, the senior manager could not agree with them. But when it was my compassionate manager speaking, the senior manager listen really carefully and agree to what he said.

How does the SCARF model apply to your Phase 0 experience so far? How will it apply to bootcamp?

SCARF = Status, Certainty, Autonomy, Relatedness, Fairness

Status: I could feel the status reward when my mastery of HTML, CSS and Javascript grew. I knew that I accidentally triggered status threat when I told Harrison that the coding task was not challenging and he promised me that I will feel sorry in phase-1.

Certainty: Let take the calculator project that I worked on as an example. I knew that the original calculator project was not difficulty for me (certainty), so I decided to stretch further to work on a more complex version of the calculator (uncertainty). After started working on the complex version of the calculator, I found that the project was more difficult than my original estimation (uncertainty). I split the work to different stages so that I could conquer them one step at a time (certainty). As more features were finished (certainty), I had less worry about the undone features (uncertainty). Eventually, I finished everything, and finally felt a sense of peace within me (certainty).

Autonomy: I would like to finish the tasks on time, every week. However, there usually got something happened in the real life that need my immediate attention. I felt helpless and stress when the thing in real life make me behind the schedule.

Relatedness: The first weekly check-in were both excited and scary. Everyone was a stranger to me (foe). I didn't feel comfortable to speak to so many strangers at once. As the strangers started sharing their stories, personal and technical, I seems to know them better (friend), and felt more at ease.

Fairness: Everyone in the bootcamp will be going through the same syllabus (fairness). But I have advantages over them due to my previous background (unfairness). I could sense that some of them need help, so I took on mentoring role to help those who needed help. (fairness)

Understanding SCARF is an important first step. It should help me to be a better team player toward my peers during phase-1 bootcamp.
