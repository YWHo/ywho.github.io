Cultural - Daily Mindfulness

18 November 2017

What did you learn from your ' mindfulness conversation'? How did the other person find it?

I tried to do 4 minutes mindfulness conversation with my family members. It was funnily awkward. My family and me have never do this kind of conversation before. Usually, we just say it when we feel like we wanted to talk. We may or may not pay attention to really listen to what the other person was saying. When it was the turn for one of my family members to say something, the person found awkward and don't know what to continue to say after 2 minutes of speaking. For the remaining time, the person choose to just silence. Being a listener, I really can feel that I did pay full attention to the person who spoke. May be because I played by the rule that I should be quiet. Or may be because I have the confidence that I will have my chance to say something when it was my turn. It was a great experiment but I suspect 4 minutes might be too long and not a practical duration for me and my family.

On next attempt, I reduced the duration to 1 minute mindfulnes. I found that this duration seems to be just right. Both speaker and listener won't feel awkward. As we took turn and switch role, I found that 1 minute was good enough for us to say what we wanted to say without pressure. Sometimes I might face the problem of feeling like I still have more things to say but the 1 minute timer was up. However, I also realise that the desire to say more was because I probably didn't use the 1 minute duration to properly expressing my thought. It was a funny experience for my family because it felt like we were playing a mini family game where everyone take turn to speak for only 1 minute.

Which of the three suggested techniques will you use to sustain your practice?

The book gave three suggestions:

  1. Have a buddy
  2. Do less than you can
  3. Take one breath a day

All three suggested techniques are good, but I personally prefer the 2nd and 3rd suggestions, because those seem very easy for me. I don't think the first suggestion is practical for my lifestyle yet.

Any other musings?

I plan to encourage my family to keep on playing the 1 minute mindfulness conversation game. I believe it will create a strong bonding and understanding for everyone in my family.
